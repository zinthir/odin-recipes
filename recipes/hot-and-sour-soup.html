Hot and Sour Soup

Description

This sour, spicy and savory soup is perfect as an appetizer or a meal of its own.

A viscous base and wide variety of textures offered by bamboo shoots, tofu, and egg make for a soup which never starts to feel samey as you scoop out seconds and thirds!

Ingredients

Yields 6-8 Servings

  • 8 cups chicken or pork stock
  • 1 ounces dried lily buds
  • 2 1/4 cups water
  • 8 ounces shiitake mushrooms, sliced thin
  • 1 small can bamboo shoots, drained and sliced into 1/4 inch strips
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 4 ounces dark chicken meat or pork, sliced into 1/2 inch stips
  • 6 ounces firm tofu, cut in 1/2 inch cubes
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon toasted sesame oil
  • 2 large eggs, whisked
  • 1/3 cup black vinegar
  • 2 teaspoob ground white pepper, ideally freshly ground
  • 4 green onions, thinly sliced

Steps

  1. Add stock, mushrooms, bamboo shoots, soy sauce, and chicken/pork to a large stockpot. Bring to a simmer on medium-high heat. While the soup is heating, use a small bowl to hydrate the lily buds in 2 cups water.
  2. Whisk the cornstarch into the water until both fully incorporate into a slurry. Once the soup is at a simmer, while stirring the soup, pour in the cornstarch slurry. Continue stirring the soup for a minute or so until it noticeably thickens.
  3. While gently stirring the soup, slowly pour in the egg to create egg ribbons. Add lily buds, discarding the water. Add tofu, sesame oil, black vinegar, and white pepper. Balance the salt, sour and heat to taste by adding additional soy sauce, black vinegar, and white pepper respectively.
  4. Serve immediately. Garnish with green onions.
